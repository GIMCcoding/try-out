Understanding the urgency

Case study farmers from Zeeland

Farmers in Zeeland, a province in the Netherlands, are facing significant challenges due to climate change. The region has experienced a temperature rise of 2 degrees Celsius since 1901, leading to more frequent and severe extreme weather events. These climatic changes have resulted in various adverse impacts on agriculture, particularly for farmers in Zeeland.

Efforts have been made at both the policy and research levels to assist farmers in adapting to climate change. Initiatives like the RIVM Dutch Climate Scenarios report provide valuable information to understand and predict climate impacts, enabling better preparation and adaptation strategies. However the financial losses experienced by the farmers in Zeeland are permanent.

The farmers in zeeland illustate a clear example why immediate policy adaption should be promoted.

Source: [https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0048969722043108]

Explore the impact

Graphs from leading sources highlighting environmental change caused by climate change

The Netherlands has seen a 2.3°C increase in average surface temperature since 1901.

Source: [https://www.iea.org/articles/netherlands-climate-resilience-policy-indicator]

Between 1910 and 2022, annual precipitation in the Netherlands increased by 26%.

Source: [https://klimaatadaptatienederland.nl/en/knowledge-dossiers/themes/waterlogging/how-precipitation-changing/]

The top graph shows monthly temperature anomalies since 1979, with red indicating warmer months and blue indicating colder months compared to the 30-year climate mean of 1980-2010. This reflects the trend of increasing warmer months over time due to global warming.


The bottom graph depicts monthly precipitation anomalies since 1979, with green representing wetter months and brown representing drier months compared to the 30-year climate mean. It helps identify shifts in precipitation patterns over time.

Source: [https://www.meteoblue.com/en/climate-change/the-netherlands_the-netherlands_2750405 ]

Analysis of OECD climate data

Temperature (Red Line): Represents the annual average temperature. An upward trend indicates increasing temperatures over the years, reflecting global warming.

Precipitation (Blue Line): Shows annual precipitation levels. Trends indicate changes in rainfall or snowfall over time.

Climate Policies (Green Line): Represents the number of climate policies adopted each year. An increasing trend suggests a growing response to climate issues.

X-Axis (Year): Shows the years for which data is available.

Y-Axis (Left): Temperature in degrees Celsius (°C).

Y-Axis (Right): Precipitation in millimeters (mm) and the number of climate policies.
